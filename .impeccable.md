## Design Context

### Users

**Public site**: A broad Nigerian audience — Lagos professionals booking leisure trips, diaspora Nigerians planning visits home or abroad, couples organising honeymoons, and corporate travel managers. They browse primarily on mobile, often in transit or during short breaks. They may be research-mode (exploratory, leisurely) or decision-mode (ready to act quickly). Both exist; the design must serve both without friction.

**Admin panel**: The ACE Voyages team — agents, managers, and a super-admin. They use it daily for lead management, tour creation, blog publishing, and dashboard KPIs. They need clarity, speed, and density over beauty.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **aspirational, warm, trustworthy**

- Aspirational: the site should make Nigerians feel like the world is accessible to them — not just for the wealthy, not intimidating, but genuinely within reach
- Warm: this is a concierge business built on human relationships and WhatsApp conversations; it should feel like talking to a knowledgeable friend, not a faceless corporation
- Trustworthy: pricing transparency, professional execution, and reliability are core promises — the design should project solidity and competence

Tagline: *Nigeria's Most Trusted Travel Partner*

Emotional goals: The user should feel **excited** (I want to go there), **confident** (these people know what they're doing), and **seen** (they understand what matters to a Nigerian traveller).

### Aesthetic Direction

**Refine the current direction — do not redesign.** The navy + gold + white palette is established brand identity and should be respected and deepened, not replaced.

- **Theme**: Light mode. This is a product browsed in daylight — on phones in offices, at home on a Sunday morning, in Lagos traffic. Light and warm.
- **Palette**: Primary Blue `#1B3A6B`, Gold Accent `#D4A017`, Background `#F8F9FA`, Dark Text `#1A1A2E`, Muted `#6B7280`. Neutral surfaces should be tinted subtly toward the brand blue — not stark grays.
- **Typography**: Inter (body), Manrope (display headings), Satisfy (script accent on hero/marketing moments). These are in use and working — the refinement goal is better hierarchy, scale, and consistency of application, not new fonts.
- **Public site feel**: Editorial warmth — photography-forward, generous whitespace, gold as a purposeful accent rather than decoration. Think quality brochure, not SaaS product.
- **Admin feel**: Functional, dense, utilitarian — but not cold. Use the same brand palette at lower saturation. Prioritise scannability and quick action over visual richness.

### Design Principles

1. **Gold is sacred.** Use `#D4A017` sparingly and purposefully — CTAs, active states, key accents. The moment it appears everywhere it loses power.
2. **Serve both mobile and desktop.** The public site is primarily mobile. Every component must be touch-friendly (48px+ targets), readable at 375px, and gracefully enhanced on desktop.
3. **Photography leads, UI supports.** Destination imagery is the hero of every page. Components frame and elevate photos rather than compete with them.
4. **Warm trust, not corporate polish.** Rounded corners, human testimonials, WhatsApp proximity — lean into the concierge quality. Avoid hard edges, sterile grids, and SaaS-style dashboard aesthetics on the public site.
5. **WCAG AA as baseline.** All text/background combinations must meet 4.5:1 contrast ratio. Interactive elements need clear focus states. Motion should respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
